Urgency Lab
Rhode Island School of Design
Spring 2019



One must continue to work for the possibility of a poetry of the future.
Gayatri Chakravorty Spivak

How do we address what’s truly urgent today? Urgent for whom? Crisis conditions have infused every aspect of culture and society, but the role and efficacy of art and design as a means for change is still debated. We’ll begin this studio course by defining what Urgency Lab is on our own terms, casting a wide view towards climate, gender, race, and oppression. Can artists and designers loosen hegemonic power? We’ll engage with a range of voices for guidance and inspiration through weekly studio visits and readings, both within and outside art and design discourse, with a particular emphasis on queer methodologies and historically marginalized perspectives. We’ll draw upon Fred Moten and Stefano Harney’s concept of the undercommons as a way to imagine Urgency Lab’s own position with an institutional context, and as the course progresses we’ll build our own “urgency network”—tools, references, modalities, and scenarios—to help us speculate, imagine, and articulate a more just futurity.

What is urgent craft? The semester will be devoted to the creation of Urgency Lab as a collective effort, so determining the Lab’s form, scope, and output together will itself be an experiment in collaborative, peer-to-peer making as an act of resistance. Working in public, radical acts of publishing, research-based exploration, rapid prototyping, non-traditional tools and platforms, and experimentation will be encouraged. Let’s subvert legacy art and design structures in order to produce new, non-normative narratives.
