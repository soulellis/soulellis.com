URGENCY READER (2019)

Urgency Reader in production (November 23, 2019)


Urgency Reader is a quick assembling of texts, risograph printed in Pawtucket, RI, and bound at the last minute to launch at the Odds and Ends Art Book Fair at Yale University Art Gallery on December 6, 2019. Suggested topics from the open call included ⊹urgency, ⊹craft ⊹queerness ⊹gender ⊹transformation ⊹kinship ⊹race ⊹survival ⊹post-apocalyptic practice ⊹futurity ⊹pedagogy ⊹surveillance capitalism ⊹death of capital ⊹radical publishing ⊹decolonization ⊹augmentation ⊹resistance ⊹sci-fi ⊹collective care ⊹joy

This volume is an experiment in publishing as an immediate gesture of call and response: the quick circulation of charged texts—in some cases raw, in-progress, or sketchy—to a small but deeply engaged audience. Eighty contributors far and wide sent 467 pages of work in just under two weeks, signaling a need to publish urgently, but less preciously, on non-corporate platforms.

I used my stapler’s max capacity to determine the page count (126 sheets + cover bound with 5/8 inch staples). The goal of the edit was to be as inclusive as possible, in some cases asking contributors to compact their submission in order to free up space for others. The order of the texts was determined randomly by assigning a series of random integers from random.org to the alphabetical list of contributors.

Urgency Reader
252 pages + cover
Risograph printed with black and fluorescent pink inks on Domtar Earthchoice paper
Staple-bound with partial wrap-around cover adhered to back page
Edition: 110 copies
