This is a two-part talk. The first part is a bit more like an artist’s talk, where I’ll introduce my work and the idea of publishing as an artistic practice
3 / 141
but where we’ll end up is somewhere here, trying to get closer to this idea of urgentcraft, and how art and design can be used to loosen power.
4 / 141
Worked as a self-taught graphic designer for many years, with my own design studio in NYC. Client-based work that focused on branding, typography, and traditional corporate identity.
5 / 141
I definitely enjoyed this work and the business was able to thrive for about 15 years.
6 / 141
But after so much time accommodating the needs of clients, I reached a limit, and felt like I needed more.
7 / 141
About 10 years ago, I really started asking questions of myself, like what do I need from design? What would it look like to call myself an artist?
8 / 141
And I was able to ask these questions by using the book form.
9 / 141
And I really started thinking about the printed book as a container for ideas. Sounds kind of obvious now, but this was totally new for me. This is a project that I did in collaboration with the John Cage Trust.
273 Relics for John Cage
10 / 141
And this one was during the London 2012 Olympics. It’s a set of 12 books about the towns of Weymouth, England and Weymouth, MA.
